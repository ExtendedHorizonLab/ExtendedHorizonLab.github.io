# Lab Handbook

Welcome to our lab! This handbook contains important information for current and prospective lab members.

## Lab Values

- **Collaboration**: We believe in working together and supporting each other
- **Inclusion**: Everyone's voice matters and diversity strengthens our research
- **Rigor**: We hold ourselves to high standards of scientific integrity
- **Impact**: Our research should make a real difference in people's lives

## Meetings

### Weekly Lab Meeting
- **When**: Fridays, 2:00 PM - 3:30 PM
- **Where**: Room 123, CSE Building
- **Format**: Research presentations, paper discussions, practice talks

### Individual Meetings
PhD students meet with their advisor weekly. Meeting times are scheduled at the start of each quarter.

## Resources

### Computing
- Lab servers are available for research computing
- Contact the lab manager for account setup

### Funding
- Conference travel is supported for students presenting papers
- Equipment purchases require advisor approval

## Expectations

### For PhD Students
- Make consistent progress on research goals
- Attend lab meetings and participate in discussions
- Submit at least one paper per year to a top venue
- Mentor junior students and contribute to lab community

### For Undergraduate Researchers
- Commit at least 10 hours per week during the quarter
- Attend lab meetings when possible
- Complete assigned tasks and communicate about challenges
- Consider applying for research funding (e.g., Mary Gates)

## Policies

### Authorship
Authorship follows ACM guidelines. Discuss authorship expectations early in any collaboration.

### Code and Data
- All code should be version controlled (GitHub)
- Data should be stored securely and backed up
- Open-source code when possible

### Publications
- All papers require advisor approval before submission
- Authors should review camera-ready versions carefully
- Acknowledge funding sources appropriately

## Contact

For questions about the lab, contact the lab manager at lab@university.edu.
